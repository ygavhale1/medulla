Know more about Body Fat and how to measure it
  • What is the purpose of body fat? Is body fat good or bad? 

    There is a common misconception that all body fat is bad. But the fact is that some body fat is needed to protect the person’s health as well as supplying a reservoir of energy for performing various body functions.

    Body Fat mass consist of two types of fat:

    1. Essential fat - is the minimal amount of fat necessary for normal physiological function. For males and females, essential fat values are typically considered to be 3% and 12%, respectively. When minimal recommended levels of essential fat drops, it may negatively affect the delivery of vitamins to the organs, and overall well-being.
    2. Nonessential fat – is above the minimal amount of essential fat. It is generally accepted that a range of 10-22 percent for men and 20-32 percent for women is considered satisfactory for good health. Non-essential body fat above the recommended range increases the risk of developing obesity-related diseases such as diabetes, high blood pressure, and even heart attack.

  • How to track your fatty food intake? 

    Dietary guidelines suggest that healthy adults should limit dietary fat to no more than 20 to 35 percent of total daily calories. To understand how many fat grams or calories you can intake, start with the number of calories you normally eat in a day. Multiply that number by the recommended percentages to get the range of fat calories you can eat each day.

    Here's an example based on a 2,000-calorie-a-day diet:

    Multiply 2,000 by .20 (20 percent) to get 400 calories There are 9 calories in a gram of fat, so you divide the number of calories by 9. Divide 400 calories by 9 (calories a gram) to get about 44 grams of fat. So if you're on a 2,000-calorie-a-day diet, 400 can come from dietary fat, which translates to between 44 grams a day.

  • What is metabolism? Why is it important?  

    Magnis dis parturient montes

    Metabolism is the process by which your body burns calories from the food you eat and drink into energy. Whether you are eating, drinking, sleeping, working etc. your body is constantly burning calories to keep you going. Metabolism is affected by body’s composition such as the amount of muscle you have versus the amount of fat. Muscle uses more calories to maintain itself than fat, hence people who are more muscular with lower percentage of body fat are said to have a higher metabolism than others who are less muscular.

  • What is BMI? 

    BMI provides a reliable indicator of body fatness for most people and is used to screen for weight categories such as underweight, normal weight, overweight and obese. Body Mass Index (BMI) is a number calculated from a person's weight and height. You can calculate your BMI as follows: weight (in kgs) divided by square of height (in meters) – Kg/m2

All you need to know about Obesity
  • What is obesity? How do I know if I am obese? 

    Obesity is a condition in which there is excess fat accumulation in the body. Obesity is commonly measured by body mass index (BMI) which calculates the relationship of weight to height. A person is considered obese if his or her BMI is above 25 according to Indian guidelines and above 30 according to WHO (World Health Organization) guidelines.

    Obesity is considered to be a condition that can have adverse effects on one’s health and increases the risk of developing health problems such as heart diseases, type 2 diabetes and more. Weight loss can help in reducing the risk of developing these health problems.

  • What is “morbid obesity” or “clinically severe” obesity? 

    For an individual to be considered clinically severe or morbidly obese he or she must have a body mass index ( BMI) above 37.5 kg/m2 without health conditions or a BMI above 32.5 kg/m2 with obesity-related health conditions, such as high blood pressure or diabetes.

Surgical options for Weight loss
  • What is bariatric surgery? 

    Bariatric surgery, also called weight-loss surgery, is a procedure that aids morbidly obese people in weight loss.

    These procedures work on one or both of the following methods:

    1. Restrictive procedures (decreases food intake by creating a smaller stomach): work by physically restricting the size of the stomach and slowing down digestion. This limits the amount of food eaten before a sensation of being full. After surgery, the stomach may at first hold as little as an ounce, although later that could stretch to 2 or 3 ounces.
    2. Malabsorptive procedures (alter the digestive process, causing food to be poorly digested and incompletely absorbed): work by changing how you take in food. In addition to restricting the size of the stomach, these surgeries physically remove or bypass parts of your digestive tract, which makes it harder for your body to absorb calories

    Please consult your surgeon for further details.

  • Is bariatric/weight loss surgery a cosmetic surgery? 

    No, bariatric/weight loss surgery is not a cosmetic surgery. Many people are of the opinion that bariatric surgery is a form of cosmetic surgery but the main difference here is that it is designed to improve many obesity related health conditions, such as type 2 diabetes and high blood pressure. On the other hand cosmetic surgery is usually undertaken for reasons of physical enhancement.

  • Will I re-gain weight post procedure? 

    Bariatric/weight loss surgery is a procedure that reduces the stomach size, allowing limited food intake or preventing complete absorption of food. This procedure helps you to feel satisfied sooner, but it does not eliminate the desire to eat. Therefore, without regular exercise and a healthy diet, it is unlikely that you will achieve your weight loss goals. Discuss the same with your surgeon or support team and plan out a program that will suit you best.

  • What is a laparoscopic surgery? 

    Bariatric/ weight loss operations may be conducted using laparoscopic techniques using instruments connected to video monitors. These allow the surgeon to view the inside of the patient’s body without having to make large incisions/cuts. Patients who undergo laparoscopic operations typically benefit from less blood loss, a shorter hospital stay, and faster recovery compared to traditional surgeries.

Types of Weight Loss Surgery
  • What is adjustable gastric banding? 

    Adjustable Gastric Band reduces the amount of food that can be eaten at one time. The band is wrapped around the upper part of the stomach, dividing the stomach into a small upper pouch that holds about ½ cup of food and a larger lower stomach. As a result, you’ll feel fuller sooner and stay fuller longer. The Gastric Band allows for normal digestion and absorption.

  • What is gastric bypass? 

    Gastric Bypass restricts food intake and the amount of calories and nutrients the body absorbs. In this procedure, the surgeon divides the stomach into a small upper stomach pouch and a larger lower stomach pouch. A section of the small intestine is directly attached to the smaller upper pouch. This allows food to bypass a portion of the small intestine. Having a smaller stomach pouch causes you to feel full sooner and eat less; bypassing part of the small intestine means the body absorbs fewer calories.

  • What are the monitoring parameters after surgery? 

    An accurate assessment of your health is needed before surgery. Consult with your surgeon about the assessment tests to be performed.

    1. Every three months for first year: CBC, glucose, creatinine

Life Style changes Post-Surgery:
  • Are there any guidelines for diet regime after the surgery? 

    Your Doctor and bariatric surgery support staff would be best to advice you on the same, however here are some of the things you can expect regarding diet during your weight loss surgery recovery period.

    Recovery in the first 2 weeks

    1. Immediately after bariatric surgery you will be on a clear liquid diet
    2. You’ll be encouraged to slowly sip at least 2 litres of low-calorie, noncarbonated fluids each day to stay hydrated
    3. Avoid alcohol and carbonated beverages

    Recovery from 3 to 6 weeks

    1. You will begin eating soft foods and if well tolerated, solid foods can be introduced about 1 month after surgery
    2. Once you can tolerate solid foods, a typical meal will include protein-rich foods, starches and whole grains, fruits, and vegetables
    3. Avoid raw vegetables, fruits with skin, nuts, popcorn, tough meats, stringy foods, crusty bread, high-sugar, high-fat, or high-calorie foods
    4. Drink at least 2 litres of low-calorie, noncarbonated fluids each day to stay hydrated
    5. Avoid carbonated beverages, alcohol, and high-calorie drinks

  • What is the typical Bariatric surgery diet? (How much food can be eaten after surgery?) 

    Your doctor is the best person to advice you about your diet post-surgery. You must be prepared for a drastic change of eating habits following your operation. Due to the small size of your new stomach pouch, you will feel full after a very small amount of food. A typical diet after gastric reduction surgery starts with clear liquids only; then broadens gradually to include any liquids, then semi-solids, then a low-fat solid diet. This should become a permanent way of eating if you wish to achieve permanent weight loss.

  • How much exercise can I do post-surgery? 

    Based on your physical condition and recovery phase, your bariatric surgery team will recommend how soon after your surgery should you start with exercise.

    first 2 weeks post-surgery

    1. To begin your exercise program, take walks or use a treadmill
    2. Use caution when bending at the waist and picking up heavy objects.
    3. Your surgeon will advise you when to return to work and when to resume prior activities.
    4. Consult with your surgeon before starting an exercise program

    3 to 6 week post-surgery

    1. Continue walking and add aerobic exercises such as cycling, jogging, and swimming.
    2. Lift objects with caution and do not perform any abdominal exercises until a member of your surgeon gives you permission to do so

    Choose physical activities that you enjoy as you will be more likely to continue doing them.

  • What are the general dietary guidelines post bariatric surgery? 

    The following are some of the generally accepted dietary guidelines a bariatric surgery patient may encounter:

    1. When eating solid food, it is essential that you chew thoroughly. You will not be able to eat chunks of meat if they are not ground or chewed thoroughly
    2. Do not drink fluids while eating
    3. Omit sweets and other high calorie foods
    4. Omit carbonated drinks, milk shakes, high-fat foods, and foods with high fiber content
    5. Avoid alcohol
    6. Limit snacking between meals
